# HR Analytics Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project presents an **HR Analytics Dashboard** created to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, and departmental trends. The dashboard helps HR teams identify high-risk attrition areas and make data-driven workforce decisions.

The analysis was performed using HR employee data and visualized using Tableau dashboards.

---

# 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze employee attrition trends
- Identify departments with high attrition
- Study age-group-wise employee distribution
- Evaluate job satisfaction across job roles
- Understand gender and education-based attrition patterns
- Provide actionable HR recommendations

---

# 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used
- Tableau
- Microsoft Excel
- Data Cleaning
- Data Visualization
- Dashboard Design

---

# 📂 Dataset Information
The dataset contains employee-related information such as:
- Employee Count
- Attrition Status
- Department
- Age Group
- Gender
- Education Field
- Job Satisfaction Ratings

---

# 📈 Key Dashboard Insights

## 1️⃣ Workforce Overview

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Employee Count | 1,470 |
| Attrition Count | 237 |
| Attrition Rate | 16.12% |
| Active Employees | 1,233 |
| Average Age | 37 |

### Insights
- Attrition rate is slightly higher than the industry benchmark.
- Workforce mainly belongs to the mid-career age segment.

---

## 2️⃣ Department-wise Attrition

| Department | Attrition Count | Percentage |
|---|---|---|
| R&D | 133 | 56.12% |
| Sales | 92 | 38.82% |
| HR | 12 | 5.06% |

### Insights
- R&D department contributes the highest attrition.
- HR department shows the lowest attrition.

---

## 3️⃣ Employee Distribution by Age Group

### Insights
- Majority of employees belong to the 30–35 age group.
- Workforce is primarily young to mid-career.

---

## 4️⃣ Attrition by Age Group

| Age Group | Attrition Rate |
|---|---|
| Under 25 | 8.44% |
| 25–34 | 18.14% |
| 35–44 | 15.61% |
| 45–54 | 3.80% |
| Over 55 | 1.27% |

### Insights
- Highest attrition occurs in the 25–34 age group.
- Younger employees are more likely to leave.

---

## 5️⃣ Gender-wise Attrition

| Gender | Attrition Count |
|---|---|
| Male | 150 |
| Female | 87 |

### Insights
- Male attrition is significantly higher.
- Certain male-dominated roles may have higher work pressure.

---

## 6️⃣ Education Field-wise Attrition

| Education Field | Observation |
|---|---|
| Life Sciences | Highest Attrition |
| Medical | Second Highest |
| HR | Lowest Attrition |

### Insights
- Employees from Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds show higher attrition trends.

---

## 7️⃣ Job Satisfaction Analysis

### Insights
- Sales Executive and Laboratory Technician roles show good satisfaction ratings.
- Some managerial and HR roles show lower satisfaction.

---

# ✅ Recommendations
- Improve employee engagement programs.
- Conduct department-specific retention analysis.
- Focus on employees aged 25–34.
- Strengthen career growth opportunities.
- Improve onboarding and mentoring.
- Conduct regular satisfaction surveys.
- Introduce work-life balance initiatives.

---

# 📌 Final Conclusion
The dashboard analysis reveals that attrition is concentrated mainly in the **R&D department**, particularly among employees aged **25–34**. Gender and education-based trends also indicate higher attrition among male employees and Life Sciences backgrounds.

By improving job satisfaction, career development, mentoring, and employee engagement, organizations can significantly reduce attrition and improve workforce stability.
